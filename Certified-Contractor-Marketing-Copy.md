## 3. Landing Page Section — Certified vs. Registered Contrast

*Slot this section below the hero/intro and above the tier pricing table. Designed to immediately qualify the visitor by license type and position Gold tier as the natural home for Certified contractors.*

---

**Headline:** Two Types of Florida Contractor's License. One Is Built for This.

---

Florida issues two classes of contractor's license, and the difference matters for public bid work.

A **Registered license** (prefixes: RG, RC, RM, RF, ER) authorizes you to work in specific counties only. If an agency two counties over posts a solicitation that's a perfect fit for your trade, you can't legally bid it — your license doesn't reach there. That's not a knock on Registered contractors. It's just the reality of the credential, and CBP's routing logic accounts for it automatically.

A **Certified license** (prefixes: CGC, CCC, CMC, CFC, EC) is a different animal. It authorizes you to work anywhere in Florida with no county restriction. Every government agency in the state is on the table. The problem isn't eligibility — it's that most Certified contractors never build a systematic process for finding and pursuing public work outside their immediate area. That's the gap CBP was built to close.

CBP's Gold tier is designed specifically for Certified contractors who want to use their statewide license as a real competitive advantage. Every week, our system scans government procurement portals across the Gulf Coast corridor and routes opportunities matched to your trade and license. When a fit appears, you get a text. Reply with your number and CBP generates the bid document, routes it for e-signature, and logs the submission. Your statewide license has been doing the hard work of qualifying you since the day you passed your exam. CBP makes sure you're actually in the room.

**CTA:** [Get Started — Gold Tier] — Statewide bid matching for FL Certified contractors.

## 4. Institutional / EDA Pitch Slide — "Local Dollars, Local Contractors"

*For: UWF EDA briefings, Gulf Coast chamber presentations, RESTORE Act / economic development conversations.*
*Framing: market failure + local economic capture, not a vendor pitch.*

---

**Slide Title:** Closing the Local Contractor Gap in Gulf Coast Public Procurement

**Slide Subtitle:** How the Certified license tier creates a statewide opportunity — and why most of it flows out of the region today

---

**Bullet Points:**

- Florida's Certified contractor license (CGC, CCC, CMC, CFC, EC) grants statewide bidding authority on public projects — every agency in FL is eligible, not just local jurisdictions

- Gulf Coast trade contractors hold these credentials in significant numbers but concentrate bids locally — not due to price competitiveness, but due to discovery friction and document preparation burden

- When local Certified contractors don't bid, awards default to larger out-of-area firms with dedicated procurement staff — local construction dollars leave the region by default, not by competition

- This is a classic market failure: the supply (qualified local contractors with statewide credentials) exists but cannot access the demand (regional public solicitations) efficiently

- CBP addresses the operational gap: automated weekly scraping of government bid portals, license-matched routing to contractors' phones, and full bid-document automation — eliminating the overhead that keeps local firms out of the room

- Pilot result (Gulf Coast corridor): one Certified Roofing Contractor, automated pipeline, weekly opportunity alerts matched to trade and license type — full bid-prep cycle handled without a dedicated staff member

- Scalable to 20–50 contractors per trade corridor on existing infrastructure — marginal cost per additional contractor is near zero after initial build

---
